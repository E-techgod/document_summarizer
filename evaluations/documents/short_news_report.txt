City Approves Pilot Program for Night Buses

The Rivergate City Council voted 6-1 on Tuesday to approve a six-month pilot program for overnight bus service on two routes connecting downtown, the hospital district, and the airport industrial park. Service will run from 12:30 a.m. to 4:30 a.m. on Fridays and Saturdays starting August 15.

Transportation staff estimate the pilot will cost $420,000, funded from the city's transit reserve and a state mobility grant. Council members said late-shift workers and airport employees had repeatedly asked for safer transportation options after midnight.

One concern raised during the meeting was ridership uncertainty. A council memo notes that a similar pilot in 2023 on a different corridor was canceled after three months because demand was weaker than expected. To avoid repeating that result, the transit department will publish monthly ridership numbers and survey riders about schedule changes.

The approved plan does not expand service citywide, and officials said any permanent program would require a separate vote after the six-month trial ends.
